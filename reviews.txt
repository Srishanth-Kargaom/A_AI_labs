I have been using the OnePlus 12 for about two months now and it has become my daily driver. The Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 chip handles everything I throw at it — gaming sessions, video editing on the go, and running multiple apps simultaneously — without breaking a sweat. The 5400mAh battery is a genuine all-day companion, and the 100W SUPERVOOC charging means I am back to full in under 30 minutes.

The Hasselblad-tuned camera system delivers punchy, well-saturated photos in daylight. Portrait mode is excellent and the colours feel natural rather than over-processed. Night photography is decent but not class-leading — the Pixel 8 still edges it out in low-light detail.

OxygenOS is a breath of fresh air compared to heavily skinned Android skins. It is clean, fast, and does not push unnecessary apps on you.

On the downside, the in-display fingerprint sensor is occasionally finicky — sometimes requiring two attempts in humid conditions. Wireless charging is also absent, which feels like an odd omission at this price point. The telephoto lens maxes out at 3x optical zoom, leaving zoom enthusiasts wanting more.

Pros:
Blazing fast performance with no throttling observed
Exceptional charging speed — 100W gets you from 0 to 100% in under 30 minutes
Clean, bloat-free OxygenOS experience
Solid build quality with an attractive design

Cons:
No wireless charging support
Fingerprint sensor reliability dips slightly in humid conditions
Telephoto zoom limited to 3x optical

Review written by Srishant K.
